# Logging by text

The fastest way to log an expense is to text it in plain English. Tally figures out the rest.

## What to send

Just describe the expense like you would to a person. Include the amount and a word or two about
what it was:

> $30 gas to client site
>
> $42 lunch with a client at Sweetgreen
>
> $120 software subscription for Figma
>
> $15 parking downtown for a meeting

Tally pulls out the **amount**, the **vendor**, and a **category**, and logs it. If everything it
needs is there, you'll get a quick confirmation and nothing more.

## Dates

By default, Tally dates an expense to the day you text it — which is usually right, because you're
logging it as it happens. If it's for a different day, just say so:

> $40 lunch on June 1
>
> drove 22 miles to the client yesterday

If you send a **photo**, Tally uses the date printed on the receipt.

## Business vs. personal

If you set a default during setup, Tally assumes that. To mark a specific one differently, just say:

> that one was personal
>
> $80 dinner, business

## One expense at a time

Send each expense as its own message — it keeps everything accurate and gives Tally a clean record
of each one. If you fire off a few in a row, that's fine; just keep them separate.

## What if Tally needs more?

Some categories (meals, travel, gifts, vehicle) need a bit more documentation under IRS rules. When
that applies, Tally asks **one** short follow-up question — see
[When Tally asks for more](/guide/substantiation). For everything else, it just logs it.

## Made a mistake?

No problem — just text a correction right after. See [Fixing & correcting](/guide/corrections).
