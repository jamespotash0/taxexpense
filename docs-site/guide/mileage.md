# Mileage

Business driving is deductible too — and Tally logs it the same way as any other expense: by text.

## Logging a trip

Just tell Tally how far you drove and where/why:

> drove 22 miles to a client meeting
>
> 35 miles to the job site and back
>
> drove 12 miles to pick up supplies yesterday

Tally records the miles and the business purpose. As with other expenses, if you don't mention a
date it uses the day you texted; say "yesterday" or a specific date to change it.

## Why the "why" matters here

Vehicle expenses are one of the categories the IRS scrutinizes most. A mileage log needs the
**date**, the **miles**, and the **business purpose** of the trip. Texting Tally as you go captures
all three in the moment — which is exactly when you remember them.

## Miles, not dollars

For mileage you give Tally the **distance**, not a dollar amount — the deduction is based on miles
driven. (How that converts to a dollar figure at tax time is something your CPA or tax software
handles using the standard mileage rate.)

::: tip
Log the trip right after you make it. A contemporaneous mileage log is far stronger documentation
than one reconstructed months later.
:::
