# Getting started

You can be logging expenses in about two minutes. There's no app to download — Tally works entirely
over text messaging.

## 1. Sign up

Head to [tallywhy.com](https://tallywhy.com) and enter your phone number. We'll text you a
6-digit code to verify it — enter that code and you're in. (That same phone-code login is how you'll
sign in to your dashboard later, too.)

## 2. A few quick questions

The first time you text, Tally asks a couple of short questions to set things up — like what kind of
work you do and whether you mostly pay from a business or personal card. This helps it categorize
your expenses correctly. It's quick, and you can change your answers later in Settings.

## 3. Log your first expense

Just text Tally like you'd text a friend. For example:

> **You:** $42 lunch with a client at Sweetgreen

Tally reads it, categorizes it, and replies with a confirmation — asking for any context it needs
(more on that in [When Tally asks for more](/guide/substantiation)).

You can also **send a photo of a receipt** instead of typing — Tally reads the vendor, amount, and
date for you. See [Receipts & photos](/guide/receipts-and-photos).

## 4. That's it

Keep texting expenses as they happen. Whenever you want to see where things stand, you can:

- **Ask by text** — "how much have I spent on meals this year?" ([Asking questions](/guide/asking-questions))
- **Open your dashboard** — review, edit, and export everything ([The dashboard](/guide/dashboard))

::: tip The golden habit
Text the expense **in the moment** — right after you pay. That's when you remember *why* it was a
business expense, and capturing the why is the whole point.
:::
