# Receipts & photos

You can snap a photo of a receipt instead of typing the details — Tally reads it for you.

## How to send a receipt

Take a picture of the receipt and text it to Tally (as a photo/MMS). Tally reads:

- the **vendor**
- the **amount**
- the **date** on the receipt

…and logs the expense, just as if you'd typed it. If anything's unclear or missing, it'll ask.

::: tip Get the best read
- Lay the receipt flat and fill the frame.
- Make sure the total and the date are visible and in focus.
- Good lighting helps — avoid heavy shadows.
:::

## Add context with the photo

You can include a note in the same message (or text one right after) so Tally captures the *why*:

> *(photo of receipt)* dinner with a client about the Q3 deal

## Do I always need a receipt?

No. For many expenses, your text alone is enough — Tally won't ask for a photo. It requests a
receipt **only when the rules require it**, mainly:

- **Any expense of $75 or more** in a strict category (meals, travel, gifts, vehicle).
- **Lodging**, where a receipt is expected regardless of amount.

For smaller expenses in those categories, **your text message is the record** — no photo needed. See
[When Tally asks for more](/guide/substantiation) for the full picture.

## Where do my receipt photos go?

They're stored privately and securely, linked to the expense. Only you can see them, through your
account. See [Privacy & security](/guide/privacy-security).
