# What is Tally?

**Tally is a text-based assistant that captures the _why_ behind your business expenses, in real
time.**

Your bank statement tells you *what* you spent — "$48 at Morton's." It doesn't tell you *why* — that
it was a client dinner about the Q3 deal. By April, that context is gone, and you're either guessing
or leaving deductions on the table.

Tally fixes that. You text it an expense as it happens — a photo of the receipt, or a quick note —
and Tally:

1. **Reads the details** (vendor, amount, date) from your photo or message.
2. **Categorizes it** under the right tax code section.
3. **Asks for context only when it has to** — the IRS requires extra documentation for some
   categories (like meals and travel), and Tally knows exactly when that applies.

Everything lands in a tidy, exportable record you can hand to your accountant or drop into
QuickBooks at tax time.

## Who it's for

Tally is built for **self-employed people** — sole proprietors and single-member LLCs — who pay for
business expenses across a mix of personal and business cards and don't have a bookkeeping system.

If you don't use business banking like Mercury or Brex, don't run QuickBooks, and don't have a
dedicated accountant tracking things month to month, Tally is for you.

## What makes it different

- **It's not "AI receipt scanning."** Plenty of apps read a receipt. Tally captures the *why* — the
  business purpose — at the moment you actually remember it.
- **It doesn't nag.** It asks for a receipt or extra detail *only* when tax rules genuinely require
  it. Most expenses are logged with zero follow-up.
- **The text is the record.** For small expenses in certain categories, your text message itself is
  valid documentation.

## What Tally is not

- ❌ It's **not tax advice.** Tally keeps records; it doesn't tell you what you owe or file your
  taxes. For specific questions, it points you to a CPA.
- ❌ It doesn't **link to your bank** (that may come later).
- ❌ It doesn't **file** anything with the IRS.
