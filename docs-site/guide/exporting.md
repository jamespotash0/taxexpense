# Exporting & your accountant

When tax time comes — or any time you want to hand things off — Tally gets your records out in the
format you need. It all happens from your [dashboard](/guide/dashboard).

## Export your records

From the dashboard you can download:

- **CSV** — a standard spreadsheet you (or your accountant) can open anywhere.
- **QuickBooks-compatible file** — formatted to import into QuickBooks.

Your export includes the details that matter for taxes: vendor, amount, date, category, the relevant
tax code section, the business purpose, and whether each expense is documentation-complete. Anything
you flagged for your CPA is marked so it stands out.

## Email your accountant

Prefer to send it straight to your accountant? Add their email in **Settings**, then you can email
your records to them in one tap from the dashboard.

You can also tee this up by text:

> email my accountant

Tally will point you to the dashboard to send it (and remind you to add their email first if you
haven't).

## Flagging things for review

If you weren't sure how to treat an expense, you can [flag it for your CPA](/guide/corrections)
— by text ("flag the $48 lunch") or in the dashboard. Flagged items are clearly marked on your
export so your accountant knows to take a look.

::: tip A clean handoff
Because Tally captured the *why* as you went, your accountant gets context — "client dinner, Q3
renewal" — not just a list of amounts. That's the difference between a smooth filing and a
back-and-forth.
:::
